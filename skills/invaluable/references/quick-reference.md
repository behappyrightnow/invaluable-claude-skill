# Quick Reference: Three-Goddess Braid Mapping

## The Template
> "My head is in **[HEAD]**. What you really get is **[HEART]**. My superpower is **[HABIT]**."

---

## Mapping Summary Table

| Dimension | Key Quality | Core Question | Primary Test | Watch For |
|-----------|-------------|---------------|--------------|-----------|
| **Heart** | Emotional, not intellectual | "What can you not not care about?" | "Yes, this is me" (recognition, not pride) | Platitudes; pointing away; no emotion |
| **Habit** | Oldest/deepest skill | "What do you do without thinking?" | Gets you in trouble *and* is your superpower | Chosen skills; recent habits; no shadow |
| **Head** | Prosaic domain | "Where have you paid the most dues?" | Prosaic, not inspirational | Sounds like mission statement; too broad |

---

## Heart: 6 Principles + 6 Tests

**Principles**: Externalize · Listen for emotion · Watch fluency · Watch platitudes · Start where you are · Contextualize in action

**Tests**:
1. It takes work (costs you something)
2. Triangulate with life decisions
3. Listen for reverence
4. "Yes, this is me" (recognition)
5. Triangulate with conditioning (what were you told was "too much"?)
6. Check directionality (toward, not away)

---

## Habit: 2 Principles + 3 Tests

**Principles**: Not a decision · Oldest and deepest

**Tests**:
1. Gets you in trouble
2. Is your superpower
3. Repeated pattern across all life domains

---

## Head: 3 Principles + 3 Tests

**Principles**: Earned the right · Drawn to it · Habit legitimized

**Tests**:
1. Prosaic, not inspirational
2. Gives clarity on strategy
3. Validates the narrative of your past

---

## Decision Analysis: 6 Elements (Ron Howard)

1. **Framing** — What problem are we actually solving?
2. **Alternatives** — What are the real options? (minimum 3)
3. **Information** — What do we know / not know?
4. **Values** — What matters, and to whom? ← *This is where the Braid lives*
5. **Integration** — How do values, alternatives, and information interact?
6. **Commitment** — Can we actually commit? If not, why not?

## Hartman's 3×3 Grid

|  | Intrinsic (life) | Extrinsic (behavior) | Systemic (rule/metric) |
|--|--|--|--|
| **Intrinsic valuation** | ✓ full person | honors the doer | humanizes the rule |
| **Extrinsic valuation** | reduces person to function | ✓ practical | applies rule practically |
| **Systemic valuation** | **ERROR**: person as metric | values behavior by metric | ✓ rule following rule |

**Common valuation mistakes**: Systemizing the intrinsic (treating people as metrics) · Intrinsicizing the systemic (treating metrics as life itself)
